# UNCTAD (UN Trade and Development) — Verified Facts Sheet

*Compiled June 2026. Each key claim carries a source URL. Membership, leadership, and the most recent conference were confirmed directly against the official unctad.org "About" page.*

---

## 1. Founding & Basics
- **Founded 1964** at UNCTAD I in **Geneva** (23 March – 16 June 1964). [unctad.org/about]
- A **permanent intergovernmental body established by the UN General Assembly**; reports to the GA and ECOSOC; part of the UN Secretariat with its own membership, leadership, and budget.
- **HQ: Geneva** (Palais des Nations); offices in New York and Addis Ababa.
- **Membership: 195 member states** (official figure — some secondary sources say 196; use **195**).
- **Why created:** emerged from **decolonization and North–South tensions**; newly independent developing countries wanted a UN forum for fairer trade and development terms, arguing GATT/IMF/World Bank under-served them.
- The **Group of 77 (G77)** was formed **15 June 1964 at UNCTAD I** by 77 developing countries.

## 2. The 2024 Rebrand — "UN Trade and Development"
- On **9 April 2024** (its **60th anniversary**) UNCTAD adopted the **brand name "UN Trade and Development."**
- **Changed:** public-facing brand name, logo, visual identity. **Did NOT change:** the **acronym "UNCTAD"** and the **legal name "United Nations Conference on Trade and Development."** It is a branding move, not a legal renaming.

## 3. Leadership
- **First Secretary-General: Raúl Prebisch** (Argentine economist), **1964–1969**; intellectual architect of UNCTAD's agenda via his structuralist **centre–periphery** analysis.
- **Current Secretary-General: Rebeca Grynspan** (Costa Rica), **since 13 September 2021** — first woman and first Central American in the role.
- *(June 2026 note: official page also lists Pedro Manuel Moreno as Deputy and Acting SG; Grynspan holds the title.)*

## 4. Mandate & the "Three Pillars"
1. **Research & Analysis** — economic research, data, statistics, flagship reports (the analytical "think tank").
2. **Consensus-Building** — intergovernmental forum where members debate and seek common positions; the quadrennial Conference + Trade and Development Board are the deliberative organs.
3. **Technical Cooperation / Assistance** — capacity-building (e.g. **ASYCUDA** customs automation, **DMFAS** debt management, investment policy reviews). 2023: ~219 projects in 74 countries, ~US$59M expenditure.

## 5. Quadrennial Conferences
- The **Conference** is the highest policy body, meeting **every four years**; the **Trade and Development Board** governs between sessions.
- **UNCTAD15:** Barbados, 2021 — "Bridgetown Covenant."
- **UNCTAD16:** **Geneva, 20–23 October 2025** (originally to be hosted by Viet Nam; moved to Geneva). Theme: *"Shaping the future: Driving economic transformation for equitable, inclusive and sustainable development."* Outcome: record participation (170 countries) and **"The Geneva Consensus"**; launched the **Sevilla Forum on Debt.**

## 6. Key Historical Contributions & Concepts
- **Prebisch–Singer thesis:** long-run **prices of primary commodities tend to fall relative to manufactured goods**, so commodity-exporting developing countries face **deteriorating terms of trade** — they must export ever more to buy the same imports. Drivers: low income-elasticity of demand for primary goods, weaker pricing power. Foundational rationale for UNCTAD.
- **Generalized System of Preferences (GSP):** **non-reciprocal, non-discriminatory tariff preferences** from developed to developing countries. Endorsed at **UNCTAD II (New Delhi, 1968)**; instituted from **1971**.
- **New International Economic Order (NIEO):** UNCTAD (with G77 + Non-Aligned Movement) was the central forum; UN GA adopted the NIEO Declaration **1 May 1974**.
- **Least Developed Countries (LDCs):** category created by the UN in **1971**; UNCTAD is the UN focal point, publishes the annual **LDC Report**; list **currently 44 countries** (peaked at 52 in 1991). Criteria: income, Human Assets Index, Economic & Environmental Vulnerability Index.
- **Debt:** long-standing work via **DMFAS**; UNCTAD16 launched the **Sevilla Forum on Debt** (2025).

## 7. Trade & Equity / Global Economic Justice Today
- **Flagship reports:** Trade and Development Report (TDR), Least Developed Countries Report, World Investment Report, Digital Economy Report.
- **Commodity dependence (State of Commodity Dependence 2025):** in 2021–2023, **95 of 143 developing economies — incl. >80% of LDCs — remained commodity-dependent**; ~two-thirds of developing countries rely on commodities for **>60% of merchandise export earnings**; all 12 South American and 46 African countries are commodity-dependent.
- **Core prescription:** **diversify and add value** rather than export raw materials, to escape volatile terms of trade.
- **South–South cooperation:** recurring priority (e.g. Global Commodities Forum 2025 on critical/energy-transition minerals).
- **Advocacy:** argues the system disadvantages developing countries; has cited market distortions costing developing countries ~US$700B/yr *(secondary source — verify before quoting)*.

## 8. Criticisms / Limitations (for balance)
- **No binding power:** unlike the **WTO**, UNCTAD does **not negotiate enforceable rules** and has **no dispute settlement**; influence is analytical/advocacy ("soft power").
- **Bloc politics:** sometimes characterised as a "club of Southern governments"; pressed to open up to civil society.
- The **2024 rebrand process** drew "flawed process" criticism from some quarters.

## Caveats
- Membership: use **195** (official).
- "Acting SG" designation currently shown alongside Grynspan's title.
- "$700 billion" stat is secondary-sourced — verify before quoting.
- GSP: distinguish "adopted 1968" vs "instituted 1971."
